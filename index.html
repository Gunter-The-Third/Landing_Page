About Me

I am Jamie Keegan-Treloar, a mathematician and PhD candidate at Flinders University. My research investigates the theoretical and computational foundations of Complex-Valued Neural Networks (CVNNs), with a particular focus on their convergence properties and representational geometry.

My current work examines the regions of the complex plane in which a single complex-valued neuron can approximate the XOR function, providing new insights into the interaction between activation function geometry, weight initialisation, and optimisation stability. This research contributes to a broader understanding of how complex-valued models can improve learning dynamics and expressivity compared to traditional real-valued networks.

Beyond theoretical analysis, I develop generalised neural network frameworks in Python that support both real and complex domains. These frameworks enable systematic exploration of activation functions, weight initialisation strategies, and gradient-based optimisation methods.

My broader interests include complex analysis, signal processing, machine learning interpretability, and statistical methods for convergence assessment. I aim to bridge the gap between mathematical theory and practical machine learning by formalising the behaviour of complex-valued systems and identifying conditions for stable learning.
